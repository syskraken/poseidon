KRAKEN PRIME — BEHAVIOR DETAILS & FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Q: Does the bot automatically end the battle when all my troops die?
A: No. There is no feature that detects your troops dying. The bot waits for
Clash of Clans' own end-of-battle screen to appear (the Return Home / claim
reward buttons), tapping the fast-forward button whenever it shows up to speed
the battle along, with a 10-minute safety timeout. When all your troops die,
Clash of Clans ends the battle by itself and shows the results screen — the bot
then taps Return Home and moves on. So it does return home shortly after your
troops are gone, but because the game ended the battle, not because the bot
counts troops. It will not surrender/end early on its own while troops are
still alive.

Q: The camera moves on its own during an attack — is something wrong? Is it ADB?
A: That is normal and intentional. The bot sends an ADB swipe (a 45-degree
diagonal "center screen" swipe) to center and frame the enemy base before it
deploys troops. This makes sure the base lines up with your pinned deploy
points. You'll see it swipe right after it picks a base to attack and again
just before deploying. It is not a bug and not a problem with ADB — it's the
bot positioning the camera.

Q: Can I run the bot on my Android phone or tablet? Does it work on Mac?
A: No. Kraken Prime is a Windows PC application. It runs Clash of Clans inside
the LDPlayer emulator on your computer and controls it through ADB. It cannot
be installed on an Android phone or tablet to farm directly on the device, and
it is not a mobile app. It is built and tested for Windows only (it installs
Python 3.11, Tesseract, and ADB for Windows). You need a Windows PC with
LDPlayer set to 1600x900 at 240 DPI. It does not officially support macOS or
Linux.

Q: What about running it on a Mac with BlueStacks (or any emulator)?
A: No — this does not work. The emulator is not the problem; the Kraken Prime
program itself is Windows-only software. Even though BlueStacks and LDPlayer
have Mac versions, the bot ships as a Windows application and installs
Windows-only Python, ADB, and Tesseract, so it cannot run on macOS no matter
which emulator you use. To run Kraken Prime on a Mac you would need Windows
itself (for example via Boot Camp on an Intel Mac, or a Windows virtual
machine), and then LDPlayer inside that Windows — a heavy setup that is not
officially supported or recommended.

Q: Can the bot request/donate troops, do Clan Wars, or Clan Capital?
A: No. Kraken Prime is a multiplayer loot-farming bot only. It attacks random
multiplayer bases for gold/elixir. It does not do war attacks, clan capital,
donations, or clan games.

Q: Does the bot upgrade buildings or train specific troops for me?
A: No. It does not manage your village, buildings, or army composition. It
retrains whatever army you already have (via the game's quick-train after each
raid) and deploys it. You choose the army; the bot just farms with it.

Q: Does Kraken Prime work with BlueStacks or other Android emulators?
A: Kraken Prime is built and tested for LDPlayer only. It connects via ADB to
127.0.0.1:5555 and relies on button templates, OCR, and timing tuned for
LDPlayer's rendering. BlueStacks or other emulators like MEmu might work if
they are set to the exact same resolution (1600x900 @ 240 DPI) and have ADB
enabled locally on port 5555, but button detection and gold reading may fail
or behave unpredictably because the UI renders differently across emulators.
Use LDPlayer for best results; other emulators are not officially supported.

Q: Can I run more than one account / emulator at once?
A: The bot connects to a single emulator at ADB 127.0.0.1:5555. It is built to
run one LDPlayer instance at a time. Running multiple emulators sharing ADB can
cause conflicts.

Q: Can I use my computer while the bot runs?
A: You can use your PC, but leave the LDPlayer emulator window open and don't
play inside it while the bot is running — the bot is tapping and swiping there.

Q: How does the bot decide which base to attack?
A: It reads the enemy's available gold with OCR and skips bases below your
Minimum Gold Target. After 20 skips in a row it attacks the next base anyway.
It only filters on gold (not elixir or dark elixir).

Q: Does it need the game in English?
A: The bot recognizes buttons by image, not text (except reading the gold
number), so button detection is language-independent. The gold OCR reads
digits only, so that is language-independent too.

Q: Is my account safe from bans?
A: Automating gameplay with any bot violates Supercell's Terms of Service and
carries a ban risk. Use it at your own risk, ideally on an account you can
afford to lose.
